What Obsidian usually looks and feels like.

Jet black volcanic glass with a smooth, glassy, highly reflective surface and conchoidal fracture pattern. In jewellery, that usually matters as much as the meaning attached to the stone. Buyers often decide faster when the stone’s visual direction already fits their taste.

Highly polished jet black beads with a glassy mirror-like surface — sleek and modern on the wrist; particularly effective in minimalist and monochrome styles.

Historically used across Mesoamerican, ancient Mediterranean, and indigenous North American cultures as a tool and ceremonial material, with associations with truth-telling and boundary-setting documented across several traditions. Gem Intent treats that background as context rather than proof. The aim is to make the stone easier to compare, not to overstate what it can do.

What to check before you buy.

Material noteBlack glass is nearly identical to obsidian visually; genuine obsidian is a natural volcanic glass and may show conchoidal (shell-like) fracture marks on unpolished edges, while manufactured glass tends to be perfectly uniform throughout.
Product detailsLook for bead size, wrist size, clasp or stretch-cord details, and close-up photography that shows the actual finish instead of only distant lifestyle shots.
Price expectationRs.200-600 / $5-18 is a useful orientation range for everyday pieces, but finish, bead size, treatment, and seller trust can move the final price.

Choosing the best format.

BraceletA strong choice when you want an easy daily reminder and the stone suits wrist wear. Check bead size and cord quality first.
RingBest when the setting protects the stone properly. Harder stones handle rings better than softer ones.
PendantUsually the safest jewellery format when you want visibility with less constant impact or rubbing.
Loose stoneUseful for gifting, desks, bedside tables, or pockets when you do not want to commit to jewellery sizing.

Care and practicality.

WaterBrief water contact is usually fine, but drying it promptly is still smart.
SunlightSunlight is usually less of an issue than abrasion, knocks, or poor storage.
Simple routineWipe with a soft cloth; obsidian is brittle despite its hardness — avoid dropping and store carefully to prevent chipping.

Where Obsidian usually works best.

Obsidian is often a sensible first look for readers focused on grounding, protection, stress relief, clarity. It can also work well when the stone’s colour and finish fit the person’s existing jewellery or gift style.

The most reliable buying decision is usually made in this order: intent first, look second, format third, listing quality fourth.

FAQ

Is Obsidian a good daily-wear option?

Obsidian can work well when the format matches its practicality. With a hardness of 5.5, the safest choice depends on whether you want a bracelet, ring, pendant, or loose piece.

What should I check before I buy Obsidian?

Black glass is nearly identical to obsidian visually; genuine obsidian is a natural volcanic glass and may show conchoidal (shell-like) fracture marks on unpolished edges, while manufactured glass tends to be perfectly uniform throughout.

How should I care for Obsidian?

Wipe with a soft cloth; obsidian is brittle despite its hardness — avoid dropping and store carefully to prevent chipping.

What kind of person usually likes Obsidian?

Obsidian is often chosen for grounding, protection, stress relief and by readers who respond to its visual mood as much as its symbolic meaning.
